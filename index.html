Price Models of San Francisco Airbnb's
What determines Airbnb prices in one of America's most expensive cities?

Location, location, location! But also, futons. Using a large data set of active SF Airbnb listings from 2019 with over 100 features, from listing price to neighborhood to the type of bedding the listing had. I used regression techniques to create a predictive model of listing prices based on the listing's features. To determine the robustness of the fitted model, I bootstrapped data points and tested predicted listing prices against actual prices. I also employed non-parametric machine learning algorithms like k-nearest neighbors to segment the data set and fine tune the price model.

key words: lasso regression, k-nearest neighbors, bootstrapping, predictive modeling

Visualizing Geometric Symmetries in Python
A side quest after one Abstract Algebra class

In Abstract Algebra, the symmetries of a polygon are the transformations that maintain sameness of the shape. For a square, its symmetries are the rotations and reflections of the shape that look the same as a non-rotated, non-reflected square. The set of all transformations that maintain the sameness of a shape forms an Algebraic Group, a mathematical system where arithmetic can be applied. Groups have multiplication tables which can be computed by composing elements of the Group with each other i.e a reflection of a square followed by a rotation. I computed the multiplication tables of squares, hexagons, and rectangles in Python, then applied heat maps to these tables to reveal even more symmetries within.
